Why instructional design?

I love the puzzle of it. Someone hands me a business problem, and my job is to dig past the surface until I find the actual knowledge or skill gap underneath, then build a learning experience that closes it. Done right, the result is more than a training. Great design helps us feel more confident, equipped, and successful, and it elevates the way we work to drive us closer to our goals.

I also love the systems behind great instructional design. I hold a PMP certification because I believe in building logical, transferrable processes that create a strong foundation for every project. A solid design system doesn't box a project in. It's what lets a designer feel free to be creative and flex to fit each learner's specific needs without falling apart under pressure.

"Sometimes in hiring decisions, you take a risk on an external hire, and you get lucky and find a Summer." Jennifer Roth-Burnette, University of Alabama Director, when considering an external applicant for a role on the Learning Commons team

That kind of trust is something I try to earn on every team I join. I'm self-sufficient and accountable, the kind of person a team can hand a high-visibility project to and know it will get done well. I'm also, at heart, a team player, and have formed wonderful professional partnerships with every team I've been part of. Independence and collaboration aren't opposites to me. They're both part of doing the work right.

I also genuinely enjoy mentoring, drawing on my academic background in instructional technology and my professional background in coaching, learning, and development. I've led teams formally as a people manager and continue to mentor informally, sharing frameworks, feedback, and hard-won knowledge with teammates who are earlier in their careers.

"Summer, can you just clone yourself?" Emily Cavender, Lyft Team Lead, when asked about filling a designer role on the Learning & Performance team

AI is a core part of how I work. I use it as a thought partner, an automator, a data analyst, a drafting tool, even a design assistant, woven into every stage of my process. But I hold my work to a quality bar AI can't meet on its own, so nothing goes out the door without my own judgment and review behind it. Learning design still needs a human hand on the wheel, and I've built my workflow around using AI ethically, efficiently, and with that standard intact.

"Summer put together an amazing training on the new Quality rubric launching soon. She was able to build eight modules in a matter of days, and it's amazing how much detail each one goes into. This is what 'customer obsessed' looks like!" Grayson Collins, Lyft Program Lead, Trust and Safety Quality

When a project is big, visible, and needs to land well, I'm the person people want on it. When something needs to move fast without cutting corners, that's also me. I design learning that elevates how people work, and I bring that same standard to every team I'm part of.
