Coaching Training
Adult Learning Theories
A CRLA-aligned training session equipping UA coaches with three foundational learning theory frameworks, Zone of Proximal Development, Multiple Intelligences, and Bloom's Taxonomy, explicitly framed around how to apply each in the dual role of tutor and coach. Closes with a guided Student Profile activity where coaches practice applying all three frameworks to a real coaching scenario before their first session.
Adult Learning Theory CRLA Certification Bloom's Taxonomy Zone of Proximal Development Multiple Intelligences Applied Practice Design
1
The Need
Coaches entering student-facing roles without a theoretical grounding for how people learn

Peer coaches were expected to facilitate skill-building sessions and run 1:1 coaching appointments from day one, but had no shared framework for understanding how students learn, where a student was in their development, or how to pitch content at the right level. Without that grounding, coaching relied on instinct rather than theory, limiting both consistency and effectiveness across the team.

2
The Learner
New and returning coaches preparing for CRLA certification

Peer coaches and professional coaching staff at the start of the academic year, many entering coaching roles for the first time. This session was part of the CRLA ITPTPC certification training sequence (Levels 1 to 3, achieved December 2022), meaning it needed to meet both practical coaching preparation standards and formal certification requirements.

3
The Approach
Three frameworks, one integrated practice activity

Covered ZPD (understanding where a student currently is relative to where they could be with support), Multiple Intelligences (how a student learns and processes information), and Bloom's Taxonomy (how to guide a student through increasingly complex levels of understanding), each framed explicitly around the tutor/coach role rather than presented as abstract theory. Closed with a Student Profile Activity: coaches read a real student profile, determined ZPD status, analyzed MI preferences, mapped Bloom's strategies, and brainstormed a full coaching session plan, applying all three frameworks in an integrated, realistic scenario before their first real appointment.

Results

This session was a required component of the CRLA ITPTPC certification sequence completed by the coaching team in December 2022. No session-specific quantitative data was collected, but the certification achievement represents formal external validation of the training program's rigor and completeness.
