Coaching Training
Growth Mindset & Combatting Imposter Syndrome
A train-the-trainer professional development workshop for UA coaches and managers, integrating Growth Mindset (Dweck), Imposter Syndrome, and Servant Leadership into a single cohesive session. Equips coaches with reframing language techniques, a six-station imposter syndrome intervention toolkit, and role-playing practice, so they can apply these frameworks directly in real coaching and management relationships. Supported by some of the most rigorous outcome data in this portfolio, including a 10.8x increase in training-related keywords found in actual coaching case notes post-training.
Train-the-Trainer Applied Psychology Behavioral Outcome Measurement Leadership Development Role-Play Design Multi-Framework Integration
1
The Need
Fixed mindset and imposter syndrome quietly undermining performance, retention, and growth

Employees with a fixed mindset avoid challenges, fear failure, and resist feedback, stalling both personal growth and organizational innovation. Those experiencing imposter syndrome doubt their accomplishments and fear exposure as frauds, leading to decreased confidence, reduced productivity, and lower engagement. Because these are psychological rather than skills-based barriers, frontline managers and coaches need specific training to recognize and address them. Generic performance coaching does not reach the root cause.

2
The Learner
Coaches and managers who needed tools, not just awareness

Peer coaches and professional coaching staff responsible for supporting the development of students and colleagues. This is a train-the-trainer audience: participants were not the direct beneficiaries of growth mindset and imposter syndrome strategies, but the intermediaries who apply these frameworks in their own coaching and management relationships daily.

3
The Approach
Six modules, three frameworks, one integrated practice architecture

Structured around six sequential modules: Introduction and Framework, Growth Mindset Reframing Language, Mindset Questionnaire, Imposter Syndrome, Raising the Ceiling of Goals, and Servant Leadership. Grounded in published research, citing Dweck's foundational mindset theory, Vergauwe et al. on imposter phenomenon in workplace contexts, and Greenleaf's servant leadership framework. The core reframing language activity used four realistic coaching scenarios (False Belief, Core Fear, Reframe) to give participants a repeatable technique they could use immediately. The six imposter syndrome intervention stations (including Fears vs. Reality, Reverse the Rabbit Hole, and Eliminate "What If?" Bias) were reinforced through role-playing practice before coaches used them in real sessions. Servant Leadership was introduced as a complementary model and connected explicitly to growth mindset through a shared goal: helping people realize their full potential.

Results

97%
considered these topics essential knowledge for coaching and management
82%

The 10.8x keyword increase was tracked by analyzing coaching appointment case notes at pre-training, 2 weeks, and 6 weeks post-training, measuring whether coaches actually used Growth Mindset, Imposter Syndrome, and Resilience language in their real sessions, not just whether they intended to. Usage data across all six imposter syndrome intervention stations confirmed sustained, distributed adoption: Fears vs. Reality (24.8%) and Reverse the Rabbit Hole (22.4%) were the two most used.
