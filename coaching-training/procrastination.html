Coaching Training
Overcoming Procrastination with Habit Systems
A professional development workshop for UA coaching staff, equipping coaches with a research-backed framework for understanding and addressing procrastination in themselves and their students. Built on the Atomic Habits Habit Loop, the session reframes procrastination as an emotionally driven habit problem rather than a willpower or character deficit, giving coaches both a personal tool and a transferable coaching conversation framework.
Behavioral Science Coach Capacity Building Train-the-Trainer Longitudinal Measurement Job Aid Design
1
The Need
Coaches lacked a framework for addressing one of students' most common presenting challenges

Procrastination was one of the most frequently cited issues in coaching sessions, yet coaches had no shared, evidence-based framework for understanding or responding to it. Without a grounded model, coaching conversations defaulted to accountability check-ins and productivity tips rather than addressing the emotional and behavioral roots of the problem.

2
The Learner
Professional coaches managing growing caseloads under real workplace pressure

Professional coaching staff and peer coaches mid-program, carrying active student caseloads while continuing their own training. Many were also experiencing the same avoidance patterns in their own professional work that they were being asked to help students address, making this session simultaneously a professional development tool and a personal one.

3
The Approach
The Habit Loop as a coaching conversation framework, not just a content model

Built around the Atomic Habits Habit Loop (Cue, Craving, Response, Reward), reframing procrastination as a solvable habit problem. Four guided questions walked coaches from "why do I procrastinate" to "how do I build habits that support productivity," with guided practice building a personal action plan. The dual design intent, personal application and coaching deployment, was made explicit: coaches left with both a completed personal plan and a facilitation model they could use with students.
