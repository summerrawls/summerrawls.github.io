Coaching Training
Stress Management & Work-Life Balance
A professional development workshop for UA coaching staff, equipping coaches with evidence-based frameworks for understanding and managing workplace stress. Teaches the eustress/distress distinction and the Surgeon General's Five Workplace Essentials, and closes with a "Self-Care Prescription" structured activity that each coach completes and commits to applying in their own professional life.
Research-Grounded Design Coach Wellbeing Mindfulness Facilitation Self-Assessment Tool Design Staff Capacity Building
1
The Need
A rapidly scaling program outpacing support infrastructure for its own coaches

As the coaching program grew, professional development for coaching staff had not kept pace. Coaches were expected to support students through stress-heavy academic periods while managing their own growing caseloads, with little structured support for their own wellbeing. Without practical, evidence-based tools for managing stress, coaches risked burnout and were less equipped to model the behaviors they were coaching others toward.

2
The Learner
Coaches under real workplace pressure, supporting students through high-stakes moments

Professional coaching staff and peer coaches mid-program, carrying active caseloads during a period of rapid program growth. These coaches were simultaneously supporting stressed students and absorbing the pressure of a program still building its infrastructure around them.

3
The Approach
A diagnostic framework and a personal commitment, not generic wellness messaging

Opened with APA 2023 Work in America Survey data to ground the session in real research rather than generic wellness framing. Taught the eustress/distress distinction as a diagnostic tool, introduced the Surgeon General's Five Workplace Essentials as an organizing framework for tracing stress back to its source domain, and distinguished mindfulness from meditation through guided practice. Closed with the Self-Care Prescription: coaches completed a self-assessment, brainstormed personalized practices, and wrote an individualized commitment designed to be revisited and updated over time.

Results

Employee opinion data on the Five Workplace Essentials showed Protection from Harm rated most important at 28.1%, followed by Work-Life Harmony at 24.6%, informing subsequent program infrastructure decisions. Multiple participants specifically cited the holistic, multi-domain framing and the practical grounding of the mindfulness exercises, with one noting they had already begun incorporating techniques into their daily routine.
