Coaching Program
The standing, campus-wide academic coaching program Summer built, staffed, and managed throughout her three years at UA. Skill Sessions delivered inside classrooms and student organizations across campus, plus opt-in 1:1 academic coaching available to any student. This is the connective infrastructure underneath every other project in this section, growing from roughly 30 sessions and 105 participants before Summer arrived to roughly 100 sessions and 3,000 participants in her third year, converting from a soft grant-funded coordinator role to a permanently budgeted Manager position.
Program Management Team Building Budget Advocacy Stakeholder Management Cross-Departmental Embedding CRLA Certification
1
The Need
A small, siloed program without the infrastructure to reach students at scale

Before Summer's tenure, academic coaching at UA was limited in scale, housed entirely within the Learning Commons, and not embedded in the broader campus ecosystem. There was no standing infrastructure to reach students at scale, no permanent budget line (the program ran on soft grant funding), and no structured training program for coaches. What existed was a starting point, not a system.

2
The Learner
Any UA student, on demand, across all colleges and class years

No enrollment requirement, no eligibility criteria, no fixed cadence. Faculty requested Skill Sessions for their classes; students booked 1:1 coaching appointments whenever they needed them. Sustaining and growing volume required making the program genuinely worth seeking out.

3
The Approach
Build the curriculum, train the coaches, embed the program, make the budget case

Summer designed and deployed the Skill Sessions curriculum, built the coach training program that made delivery at scale possible, and managed the full staffing pipeline from recruiting and onboarding to year-over-year retention. She scaled delivery from the Learning Commons into classrooms, student clubs, academic departments, and eventually the QEP pilot, then used the resulting growth and retention data to convert the program from a grant-funded coordinator role to a permanently budgeted Manager position after 1.5 years.
