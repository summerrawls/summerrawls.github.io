Retention Programs
Quality Enhancement Plan
A competitive institutional proposal co-authored by Summer in 2023 to establish the University of Alabama's first campus-wide Supplemental Instruction program, submitted as part of UA's decennial SACSCOC reaccreditation cycle. Selected from a competitive pool by a committee including the Provost and Deans from all eight academic colleges. Summer collaborated on the needs analysis, assessment plan, program design, and proposal writing, and contributed to the selection presentation deck. The program launched in 2023 to 2024 and reached 25 to 39% enrollment adoption in supported courses within its first year.
Institutional Proposal Authorship Needs Analysis Multi-Year Assessment Design Budget and Staffing Planning Governance Stakeholder Management Program Design
1
The Need
High DFW rates in historically difficult courses, and no peer-led support model to address them

UA lacked a Supplemental Instruction program despite documented high drop, fail, and withdrawal rates in high-enrollment gateway courses. As part of its decennial SACSCOC reaccreditation cycle, the university needed to propose and defend a data-driven academic support model capable of measurably improving course completion, retention, and students' sense of academic belonging.

2
The Audience
Two audiences: students in high-DFW courses, and a governance committee that had to be convinced

The end beneficiaries were undergraduate students enrolled in historically difficult courses who would receive peer-led SI support once the program launched. The immediate audience was the institutional review committee, including the Provost, Deans from all eight academic colleges, faculty, staff, and students, who evaluated the proposal's rigor, feasibility, and projected impact in a competitive selection process.

3
The Approach
A data-grounded needs analysis, a four-goal assessment plan, and a six-year budget and staffing model

The team conducted a comprehensive needs analysis using historical DFW rates, GPA trends, and retention data from 2016 to 2022 to identify the highest-need courses and student populations. The proposal structured the program around four goals spanning course completion, retention among at-risk populations, proactive student engagement, and academic belonging, each paired with baseline data, annual benchmarks, and six-year targets. Summer collaborated on building the long-range staffing model and multi-year budget plan, and adapted the Supplemental Instruction model from the International Center for SI to UA's specific institutional context.
