Retention Programs
Skill Sessions Curriculum
A student-facing academic skill-building curriculum built from scratch and scaled across all four retention programs at UA. Over 20 sessions were designed or adapted, covering note taking, study skills, time management, procrastination, stress management, and more. Two sessions are featured here as examples of the curriculum's design approach and the job aids and evaluation data they produced.
Curriculum Design at Scale Cognitive Learning Science Bloom's Taxonomy Multi-Method Instruction Stakeholder Partnership Pre/Post Data Collection CRLA Certification
1
The Need
No structured academic skill-building curriculum existed for UA students

When Summer joined, academic coaching at UA was informal and small in scale. Students lacked access to research-backed strategies for note taking, studying, time management, procrastination, stress, and mindset, with no consistent, scalable way to access this kind of instruction across campus. Improving retention required addressing those habits directly, not just tracking them.

2
The Learner
Students across four retention populations, from scholarship risk to GPA recovery

Undergraduate students ranging from scholarship recipients at continuation risk (Uplift) to freshmen below a 2.0 GPA (Tide Watch) to general coaching participants. Most had never been explicitly taught structured academic strategies and were relying on passive or underdeveloped habits without awareness that evidence-based alternatives existed.

3
The Approach
20+ sessions built and deployed at scale, each grounded in evidence-based frameworks

Every session followed a consistent instructional architecture: introduce a concept, model it, have students practice it live, then debrief as a group. Sessions were designed for flexibility across class sizes and contexts, adapted for specific student populations (including an engineering-focused version of Advanced Study Skills built with a College of Engineering faculty partner), and later adapted into professional development versions for coaching staff. The full curriculum was certified by the College Reading and Learning Association at all three levels of its ITPTPC certification in December 2022.
