Retention Programs
Tide Watch
A campus-wide retention program for second-semester freshmen at the University of Alabama with a GPA below 2.0. Eligible to roughly 700 students spanning every academic college, with approximately 350 participating. Used the same Skill Session onboarding plus monthly coaching model as Uplift, but required coordination across a significantly broader, more horizontally distributed stakeholder group: departmental deans and First Year Experience directors across every college rather than a single scholarship office chain.
Campus-Wide Program Management Multi-College Stakeholder Coordination Voluntary Engagement Design Program Replication at Scale
1
The Need
A large, dispersed at-risk population with no single administrative owner

A sizable population of second-semester freshmen were finishing their first full year below a 2.0 GPA, a marker strongly associated with attrition risk. Unlike Uplift's scholarship-tied population, this group spanned every college at the university and wasn't defined by a single funding or eligibility mechanism, making it both larger in scale and more structurally complex to coordinate.

2
The Learner
~700 eligible freshmen across all UA colleges, with no enrollment requirement

Second-semester freshmen below a 2.0 GPA spanning every college at the university. Unlike Uplift, participation was not compulsory, which made engagement rate a genuine program management metric rather than a baseline assumption. Approximately 350 of 700 eligible students participated, around 50%.

3
The Approach
A proven program structure applied at significantly larger scale, with a new stakeholder model

Used the same structure as Uplift: weekly Skill Sessions for the first four weeks, followed by monthly 1:1 coaching for the remainder of the semester. Where Tide Watch differed was in stakeholder architecture: rather than a single scholarship-office chain, coordination spanned departmental deans and First Year Experience program directors across every academic college, requiring outreach and relationship management across many more units simultaneously.

Results

Tide Watch's launch in 2023 to 2024 coincided with the largest year-over-year jump in program history: Skill Session participation rose from roughly 1,500 to 3,000, and 1:1 coaching volume rose from roughly 1,200 to 3,000 annual appointments. The approximately 50% participation rate for a non-compulsory, campus-wide voluntary intervention is itself a meaningful program management result.
